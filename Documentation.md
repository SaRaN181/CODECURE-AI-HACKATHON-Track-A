# 🧪 CodeCure: Multi-Pathway Toxicity AI Diagnostic Engine
## Comprehensive Project Documentation & Technical Report

---

### 📋 Executive Summary
**CodeCure** is a state-of-the-art AI-driven pharmacology dashboard designed to accelerate early-stage drug discovery. By leveraging a high-performance ensemble of 12 Machine Learning models, CodeCure provides instantaneous toxicological profiling of chemical compounds from simple SMILES strings. It bridges the gap between raw chemical data and biological insight, focusing on both **Nuclear Receptor (NR)** interactions and **Stress Response (SR)** pathways.

---

### 🏗️ 1. System Architecture & Workflow

The system follows a modular pipeline designed for speed, accuracy, and interpretability.

#### **Data Flow Diagram**
> <img width="708" height="226" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/94636004-5185-4eca-ad85-7f50f89d3b38"/>

#### **Phase 1: Input & Pre-processing**
- **Input:** 1-D SMILES (Simplified Molecular Input Line Entry System) string.
- **Validation:** Real-time sanitization using RDKit to ensure chemical validity.

#### **Phase 2: High-Dimensional Feature Extraction**
We transform the chemical structure into a 2,051-dimensional vector:
| Feature Category | Dimension | Method | Purpose |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Structural Topology** | 2048-bit | Morgan Fingerprints (Radius 2) | Maps circular environment around atoms to capture functional groups and motifs. |
| **Lipophilicity** | 1-bit | Wildman-Crippen LogP | Predicts solubility and membrane permeability. |
| **Molecular Mass** | 1-bit | Exact Molecular Weight | Fundamental physical property for drug-likeness. |
| **Drug-likeness** | 1-bit | QED (Quantitative Estimate) | Holistic score of "druggability" based on multiple descriptors. |

#### **Phase 3: The 12-Model Diagnostic Ensemble**
Instead of a single binary classifier, we use 12 independent **XGBoost** models trained on Tox21 challenge data:
- **7 Nuclear Receptor Assays:** NR-AR, NR-AR-LBD, NR-AhR, NR-Aromatase, NR-ER, NR-ER-LBD, NR-PPAR-gamma.
- **5 Stress Response Assays:** SR-ARE, SR-ATAD5, SR-HSE, SR-MMP, SR-p53.

### 📉 3. Technical Performance: Pros & Cons

#### **✅ Advantages (Pros)**
- **Computational Efficiency:** Predictions are delivered in <500ms, compared to months for traditional laboratory assays.
- **Explainable AI (XAI):** Unlike deep learning "black boxes," our ensemble uses gradient boosting, allowing for clear feature importance mappings.
- **Mechanism Specificity:** Identifies *why* a compound is toxic (e.g., "Mitochondrial Damage" vs "Androgen Receptor binding").
- **Local Persistence:** Built-in history tracking saves every analysis locally for downstream research review.

#### **❌ Current Limitations (Cons)**
- **Geometric Blindness:** The model uses 2D topology and doesn't fully account for 3D stereochemistry or atomic charges.
- **Metabolic Gap:** Does not predict toxicity of potential metabolites formed in the liver (*In Vivo* vs *In Vitro* discrepancy).
- **Assay Noise:** Inherits experimental noise from the baseline Tox21 datasets (e.g., autofluorescence interference).

### ⚖️ 4. Decision Logic & Risk Assessment

The system uses a weighted voting mechanism to ensure safety-first results.

| Risk Level | Visual Cue | Scientific Insight |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Severe** | 🚨 | High signal in both Receptor binding (NR) and Cellular Stress (SR). High danger. |
| **High** | 🔴 | Significant activation of cellular stress pathways (e.g., DNA damage, P53). |
| **Moderate** | 🟡 | Receptor-level interactions detected; compound may be an endocrine disruptor. |
| **Low** | 🟢 | Molecule falls within the safety distributions of the ZINC250k dataset. |

### 📜 5. Workflow & History Tracking
The system now includes a robust **Audit Trail**.
- **Local Storage:** `history.csv` records every SMILES, Timestamp, and Prediction.
- **Session Continuity:** The Dashboard pre-loads history on launch, allowing for seamless multi-molecule comparisons.
